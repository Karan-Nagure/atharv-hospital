Our Story

Born out of a Vision to Serve

Atharv Hospital was founded in 2002 by Dr. A.B. Kulkarni with a single mission: to provide the kind of healthcare to the people of Gokak that was previously only accessible in large cities. What began as a 20-bed facility has grown into a 500-bed multi-specialty hospital serving over 50,000 patients a year.

We believe healthcare is a right, not a privilege. Our pricing is transparent, our care is uncompromised, and our commitment to the community runs deep.

  • Multi-specialty hospital with 12+ departments
  • 500-bed facility with advanced ICU & NICU
  • Blood bank, pharmacy, and diagnostic lab on-site

📞 Call Us

What are your OPD timings?

▾
Our OPD is open Monday to Saturday, from 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM. Our Emergency department operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, including public holidays.

How do I book an appointment?

▾
The easiest way is to send us a WhatsApp message on +91 831 222 3344. You can also call us directly. Please mention your preferred doctor, department, and date, and we'll confirm your slot within 30 minutes.

Do you accept cashless insurance?

▾
Yes. We accept cashless treatment under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY), Kalayana Karnataka Arogya Karnataka, and most major private health insurance policies. Please carry your insurance card and ID proof.

Is a blood bank available at the hospital?

▾
Yes, we have a 24-hour licensed blood bank within the hospital premises. Blood components are available for all groups. For emergency requirements, please call our helpline directly.

Do you provide ambulance services?

▾
Yes, we have dedicated ambulances equipped with life support systems available round the clock. You can also dial 108 (government emergency service). Our hospital team will coordinate on arrival.

Are reports available online?

▾
Yes. Lab and diagnostic reports can be sent to you via WhatsApp or email. Simply provide your mobile number or email when registering at our reception. Reports are typically ready within 4–6 hours.

What facilities are available for inpatients?

▾
We offer general wards, semi-private rooms, and private suites. All rooms have attached bathrooms, nurse call systems, and diet catering. Wi-Fi is available in private and semi-private rooms.

Do you have a pharmacy?

▾
Yes, our in-house pharmacy is open 24/7 and stocks a comprehensive range of medications at affordable rates. Generic equivalents are also available on request to help reduce costs.

First Aid Guide

Basic First Aid – What to Do

While awaiting emergency care, these steps can make a critical difference. Always call 108 first.

❤️ Cardiac Arrest

  1. Call 108 immediately
  2. Begin CPR — 30 chest compressions, 2 rescue breaths
  3. Use AED if available
  4. Do not stop until help arrives

🧠 Stroke (FAST)

  1. Face drooping? Arm weakness? Speech slurred?
  2. Time to call 108 immediately
  3. Keep patient calm and still
  4. Note time symptoms started

🩸 Severe Bleeding

  1. Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth
  2. Elevate the injured limb if possible
  3. Do not remove embedded objects
  4. Call 108 and keep patient warm

🔥 Burns

  1. Cool with running water for 10–20 minutes
  2. Do not use ice, butter or toothpaste
  3. Cover loosely with a clean bandage
  4. Seek hospital care for large or deep burns

😮 Choking (Adult)

  1. Encourage the person to cough forcefully
  2. Give 5 firm back blows between shoulder blades
  3. Give 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich)
  4. Call 108 if obstruction doesn't clear

🦴 Fracture / Bone Injury

  1. Do not attempt to straighten the limb
  2. Immobilise using splints or rolled cloth
  3. Apply ice pack wrapped in cloth for swelling
  4. Transport to hospital carefully
